Dior Fall 2026: Sun King

Summary

Jonathan Anderson presented Dior's Fall 2026 collection at the Tuileries Garden, reinterpreting 18th-century codes in a modern way. Deconstructed frock coats, peplum jackets, and bustle skirts were presented in candied almond shades of Chantilly lace and metallic jacquards.

Implications

Anderson noted that "there are no permanent lines anymore" and mentioned the reality that 50 million aspirational consumers have left the market due to excessive price increases post-pandemic. He emphasized small-batch production to improve quality, reflecting luxury brands' need to target the 1%.

✦ "The sun shines on everyone" — Whether Anderson's aristocratic codes will work in a luxury market that has turned its back on aspirational consumers remains to be seen, but his focus on craftsmanship is clear.

Tom Ford Fall 2026: Sobering Up

Summary

Haider Ackermann's Tom Ford Fall 2026 collection featured pinstripes, white shirts, and military-inflected tailoring. In the minimalist show space at Place Vendôme, Ackermann spoke about "standing straight in life, facing everything that's happening in this world."

War in the Gulf Tests Resilience of a Rare Bright Patch for Luxury

Summary

Escalating war between the US-Israel and Iran is shaking the Middle East's luxury retail environment. Dubai and Abu Dhabi have been rare "bright spots" for luxury, attracting wealthy expatriates. However, recent strikes have closed airports, disrupting regional business activity.

Implications

This crisis shows how quickly geopolitics can disrupt carefully cultivated luxury retail hubs. The Middle East has served as a rare oasis of stability amid uncertainty. With wealthy consumers cutting spending, instability in this region could be another blow to luxury brands.

✦ Middle East retailers report customers are coming to "safer Dubai," presenting the interesting paradox that regional shopping may be seen as a safer alternative than Europe or the US.
